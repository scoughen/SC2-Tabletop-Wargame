Scenarios

Different victory conditions and gameplay modes

1. Annihilation
Time: 45-60 min Complexity: Low

Victory Conditions

  • Primary: Destroy enemy's Main Base Building (instant victory)
  • Secondary: Most Victory Points at Round 10

Victory Points

  • Enemy Main Base Destroyed: Instant victory
  • Destroy Production Building: 2 VP each
  • Destroy Tech Building: 3 VP each
  • Kill 10 Supply Worth of Units: 1 VP
  • Control Expansion at game end: 1 VP

Setup

  • Standard 3' × 3' board with 5 expansion locations
  • Both players deploy in opposite corners
  • Starting resources: 200 Minerals, 0 Gas

Special Rules

  • Total War: No restrictions on targeting buildings
  • Decisive Strike: Destroying enemy main base ends game immediately
  • Harassment Matters: Killing workers counts toward supply VP
Strategic Notes: Most straightforward scenario - pure military conflict. Defensive structures critical to protect main base. Balance between offense (kill their base) and defense (protect yours).
2. Control Points
Time: 50-60 min Complexity: Medium

Victory Conditions

  • Primary: Control 3+ Control Points at end of any round (instant victory)
  • Secondary: Most Victory Points at Round 10

Victory Points

  • Control Point Held: 2 VP per round
  • Destroy Enemy Building: 1 VP
  • Kill 10 Supply Worth of Units: 1 VP

Control Points (5 total)

  • Center Expansion (18", 18")
  • Player 1 Mid-Field (12", 24")
  • Player 2 Mid-Field (24", 12")
  • Player 1 Forward (6", 30")
  • Player 2 Forward (30", 6")

Controlling a Point

  • Have more models within 6" than opponent
  • Workers count for control
  • Measured during Production Phase
  • Contested if equal numbers (no VP awarded)
  • Base building counts as 5 models for control
Strategic Notes: Rewards map control over pure combat power. Mobile units excel. Defensive structures can "hold" points cheaply. Expanding to control points serves dual purpose.
3. Resource Rush
Time: 45-55 min Complexity: Medium

Victory Conditions

  • Primary: First to accumulate 1000 total minerals collected wins
  • Secondary: Most resources collected by Round 10

Resource Tracking

  • Track total resources collected separately from current stockpile
  • Spending resources doesn't reduce victory progress
  • Example: Collect 500M, spend 300M → 500M toward 1000M goal

Setup

  • Standard 3' × 3' board with 7 expansion locations
  • Modified Starting Resources: 100 Minerals, 0 Gas (encourages faster expansion)

Economic Targets by Round

  • Round 3: ~250M collected
  • Round 5: ~450M collected
  • Round 7: ~700M collected
  • Round 10: 1000M (victory threshold)
Strategic Notes: Defensive players can thrive. Worker production and expansion timing critical. Harassment attacks very effective. Military just needs to defend, not dominate.
4. Assassination
Time: 30-45 min Complexity: Medium

Victory Conditions

  • Primary: Kill enemy Hero Unit (instant victory)
  • Secondary: Most Victory Points at Round 10

Victory Points

  • Kill Enemy Hero: Instant victory
  • Wound Enemy Hero: 1 VP per wound dealt (cumulative)
  • Destroy Building: 1 VP
  • Kill 10 Supply Worth of Units: 1 VP

Hero Selection

Each player chooses ONE hero at game start. See faction pages for hero stats:

  • Terran: Jim Raynor, Sarah Kerrigan
  • Protoss: Artanis, Zeratul
  • Zerg: Zagara, Abathur

Setup

  • Standard 3' × 3' board
  • Each player deploys chosen hero in deployment zone
  • Starting resources: 200 Minerals, 0 Gas
  • Hero starts adjacent to main structure
Strategic Notes: High-risk, high-reward. Assassination attempts vs hero protection. Heroes powerful but vulnerable. Cloaked heroes require detection.
5. Hold the Line
Time: 50-60 min Complexity: High

Players

One player is Defender, one is Attacker (decide randomly or by agreement)

Victory Conditions

Defender Wins If:
  • Prevents Attacker from destroying all 3 Objectives by Round 10
  • OR destroys Attacker's main base
Attacker Wins If:
  • Destroys all 3 Defender's Objectives before Round 10 ends
  • OR destroys Defender's main base

Defender Setup

  • Deploys in one corner (12" × 12" deployment zone)
  • Starting Resources: 300 Minerals, 100 Gas (extra for defense)
  • Starting Army: 500 points of units + buildings
  • Objectives: Places 3 objective buildings in deployment zone
    • Each objective: Armor Save 4+, 15 Wounds
    • Cannot move, cannot attack

Attacker Setup

  • Deploys in opposite corner
  • Starting Resources: 200 Minerals, 0 Gas (standard)
  • Starting Army: 700 points of units + buildings (larger force)
  • Reinforcement Advantage: New units deploy from board edge nearest Defender
Strategic Notes: Defender builds defensive structures. Attacker pushes aggressively with superior numbers. Siege units excel for Attacker. Defender funnels attackers through choke points.
6. Escalation
Time: 60-75 min Complexity: High

Victory Conditions

  • Primary: Most Victory Points at Round 10
  • Tiebreaker: Control majority of expansions

Victory Points

  • Destroy Building: 2 VP
  • Control Expansion: 1 VP per round
  • Kill 10 Supply Worth of Units: 1 VP
  • First Blood: Kill first enemy unit = 1 VP (one-time)
  • Tech Advantage: First to reach Tier 3 tech = 2 VP (one-time)

Escalation Phases

Phase 1: Early Game (Rounds 1-3)

  • Resource Modifier: Standard income
  • Restrictions:
    • Cannot build Tier 3 units
    • Maximum 1 expansion allowed
    • Focus on basic infantry and economy

Phase 2: Mid Game (Rounds 4-7)

  • Resource Modifier: +50% income (multiply all resource generation by 1.5×)
  • Restrictions Lifted:
    • Tier 2 and Tier 3 units available
    • Maximum 3 expansions allowed
    • Tech buildings unlocked

Phase 3: Late Game (Rounds 8-10)

  • Resource Modifier: +100% income (double all resource generation)
  • Restrictions: None
  • Max Expansions: Unlimited

Tier Restrictions

  • Tier 1 (Rounds 1-3): Marines/Zealots/Zerglings, Reapers, Queens, Probes/SCVs/Drones
  • Tier 2 (Rounds 4+): Marauders, Hellions, Medivacs, Vikings, Roaches, Hydralisks, Mutalisks, Stalkers, Sentries, Phoenix, Immortals
  • Tier 3 (Rounds 4+): Siege Tanks, Thors, Banshees, Ravens, Banelings, Ultralisks, Corruptors, Brood Lords, Colossi, Void Rays, High Templar, Archons
Strategic Notes: Early game focuses on economy. Mid game rewards tech investment. Late game sees massive battles with elite units. Income doubling makes reinforcements fast.
